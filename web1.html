A dual-blockade FDC targeting the key inflammatory pathways for upper and lower airway disease.

Indication 1: Allergic Rhinitis (AR)

For AR patients, especially those with nasal congestion, monotherapy often falls short. The FDC provides a dual blockade to control the full spectrum of symptoms.

Levocetirizine (H1-Blocker): Targets histamine, the primary driver of acute symptoms like sneezing, rhinorrhea, and pruritus.

Montelukast (LTRA): Targets leukotrienes, the key mediators of late-phase inflammation responsible for persistent nasal congestion (DrugBank).

Clinical Proof: Superior Efficacy

  • A Phase III, randomized, double-blind trial found the Montelukast + Levocetirizine FDC showed a **statistically significant greater improvement** in daytime nasal symptom scores vs. monotherapy (Panchal S, et al. 2021).
  • A 2024 meta-analysis confirmed the FDC provided significantly better outcomes for **nasal obstruction** (congestion) compared to monotherapy (Eur Ann Allergy Clin Immunol. 2024).
  • In pediatric patients (6-14 yrs), the FDC was significantly more effective than montelukast alone at reducing **daytime nasal congestion (p=0.0341)** (Kim CK, et al. 2021).

Indication 2: Asthma

The **Montelukast** component directly addresses the underlying pathophysiology of asthma as a Leukotriene Receptor Antagonist (LTRA).

Cysteinyl leukotrienes (CysLTs) are potent pro-inflammatory mediators that cause bronchoconstriction, airway edema, and mucus secretion. By blocking the CysLT1 receptor, Montelukast provides anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects.

It is recommended in **GINA guidelines** as an adjunctive or alternative anti-inflammatory therapy for mild-to-moderate persistent asthma (GINA 2024).

Clinical Proof: Symptom Control

  • In a study of adult patients with mild-to-moderate persistent asthma, 4 weeks of add-on Montelukast treatment demonstrated significant clinical improvements:
  • **83.3% improvement** in exercise tolerance
  • **80% improvement** in early morning wakening
  • (Al-Rubaish A, 2014)

Indication 3: The AR-Asthma Overlap (Unified Airway)

This is where the FDC provides its most significant advantage. Studies show that a high percentage of patients with asthma also suffer from AR. Both conditions are manifestations of a "Unified Airway Disease."

The **ARIA guidelines** explicitly recommend treating AR in asthmatic patients to improve asthma control (Bousquet J, et al. 2020).

Montair LC is uniquely positioned to treat both the upper (AR) and lower (Asthma) airway inflammation simultaneously.

Clinical Proof: Managing Comorbidity

  • Leukotrienes are the common inflammatory pathway for *both* AR nasal congestion and asthmatic bronchoconstriction.
  • A retrospective study on patients with asthma and comorbid seasonal AR found that adding montelukast to their therapy **significantly improved long-term asthma control** and led to **substantial reductions in asthma-related resource use** (Price D, et al. 2018).
